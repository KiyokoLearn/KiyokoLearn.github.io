In English, we always have to include the subject. "I go to school. I go to bed. I have a red dog." However, in Japanese, if the subject is understood, Watashi can be dropped.






















Irregulars

Irregulars
8 years old はっさい
20 years old はたち
When refering to age, you do not use hachisai, juuhachisai, and so on. Instead you use hassai, juuhassai, etc.
To say にじゅうさい is perfectly fine. However, a special word is given to 20 years old because it is considered to be coming of age. When using はたち, you do not use ～さい with it.

Grammar

Recall that wa is the topic marker and usually goes after the subject.
When using the verb "to be" (Is/Am), the typical sentence structure is:
A is B = _______ wa ______ desu
For example, if I wanted to say, I am a student. You would write わたしはがくせいです。
