There are two different words for the numbers 4 and 7. Which word you use depends on what you are speaking about. For example, with 7, when talking about age you would use なな, but when talking about time, you would use しち. When to use which version of the number will be discussed later. For counting purposes right now, just use よん and なな.
よん and きゅう are sometimes shortened to よ and く depending on what you are speaking about. Again, those will be dealt with when we come to them. For now just use よん and きゅう.
In English, put the comma after the 3rd zero. In Japanese, the comma would go after the 4th zero. For more info on the comma placement, please see the table below.
