Lesson 7 -- Well Hello There!! (Greetings and More)


Vocabulary
Good Morning おはよう、 おはよございます
Good Afternoon こんにちわ
Good Evening こんばんわ
Good Night おやすみ、　おやすみなさい
Good Bye さようなら、じゃあね、またね、またあした
Thank You ありがと、ありがとございます
Excuse Me すみません
I'm Sorry ごめん、ごめんなさい
I'll go and come back いってきます
Please go and come back いってらっしゃい
I'm home ただいま
Welcome home おかえり、おかえりなさい
How do you do? はじめまして
Nice to meet you よろしくおねがいします



Adding ございます to words such as おはよう　and ありがとう make them more polite.
さようなら is used for long goodbyes. If you will see the person the next day you can use またあした as "See you tomorrow" For general goodbyes you can use じゃあね or またね.
すみません can also mean "I'm sorry" and "Thank you." If you drop your pen and somebody picks it up for you, you can use すみません. すみません can also be used if you were to accidentally bump into somebody or step on their foot and such.
いってきます is what you say when you are leaving the house
いってらっしゃい is said by whoever is seeing you off.
はじめまして and よろしくおねがいします are used together when being introduced or introducing yourself for the first time.












Vocab In Use

  • I'm ready to leave for school. As I head to the door, I call back to my mom, "いってきます."
  • She says back, "いってらっしゃお."
  • As I'm walking to school, I pass my neighbor, and I greet her with "おはようございます."
  • I am the new girl at school. The teacher introduces me to the class and I greet my classmates with "はじめましてよろしくおねがいします."
  • As I walk to my seat, I accidentally bump into one of the desks and I say, "すみません."
  • One of the other girls shows me around school and I thank her with "ありがとうございます."
  • After school around 3 o' clock, I see my neighbor again and say, "こんにちわ."
  • I'm finally home and when I walk in the door, I announce, "ただいま."
  • My mother greets me with, "おかえり."
  • Later that evening I go to the convenience store and run into my teacher. I greet him with "こんばんわ."
  • After I return from the convenience store, I watch television with my family and then wish them goodnight, "おやすみ."
